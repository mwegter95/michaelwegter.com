How CivicQ scores the record

Every score on CivicQ, for a representative, a bill, or a public statement, is built from the same six weighted dimensions. This page is the full, published explanation. Nothing here is hidden or adjusted case by case.

Select a dimension above to see its full description.

The six dimensions, in detail

Weights sum to 100% and are applied identically across every representative, bill, and statement CivicQ scores. Changing a weight requires a published revision note, dated and archived.

Source Verification 25%

The highest-weighted dimension. A record cannot be published until it traces to a primary government or official source document, a roll call, a bill text, a session transcript. Secondhand summaries are never treated as a source.

Vote Record Accuracy 20%

Roll-call votes are cross-checked directly against the official legislative record. A discrepancy of even one vote triggers a manual review before publication.

Bill Activity Completeness 18%

A bill's record must reflect every stage, introduction, committee referral, amendments, floor votes, and final outcome. Partial tracking lowers this score until the gap is filled.

Statement Sourcing 15%

Public statements are only published with a dated, original recording, transcript, or official press release attached. Paraphrased or unattributed quotes are excluded.

Update Timeliness 12%

Measures the lag between a real-world change, a new vote, a bill status update, and that change appearing in CivicQ's record. Faster, verified updates score higher.

Methodology Transparency 10%

This page is itself part of the score: the weighting above is published in full, with no undisclosed criteria, and any revision is dated and archived for comparison.
